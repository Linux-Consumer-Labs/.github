# Linux Consumer Labs

**Researching ways to reach "Just Works" on the Linux desktop.**

Linux Consumer Labs is an open research organization exploring how Linux can deliver the reliability, polish, and low-friction experience that consumers expect — without abandoning what makes Linux valuable.

## Mission

We study the gap between what Linux *can* do and what everyday users *experience*. Our work is deliberately exploratory: we ask uncomfortable questions, borrow ideas from other platforms when they work better, and prototype paths forward that respect the kernel rather than fight it.

## Philosophy

| Principle | What it means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Exploring What-Ifs** | We treat bold hypotheses as first-class research. "What if we did X instead of the traditional Linux way?" is a valid starting point. |
| **Learning from Other OSes** | macOS, Windows, ChromeOS, and mobile platforms solve real problems well. We study those solutions honestly — not to copy blindly, but to understand *why* they work. |
| **Not Strictly Following Linux Practices** | Convention is a data point, not a constraint. If the established Linux approach creates friction for consumers, we document it and explore alternatives. |
| **Working Alongside the Kernel** | We do not fight the Linux kernel. We build on its strengths, accept its boundaries, and design userland solutions that complement rather than undermine it. |

## What We Do

- **Research briefs** — Structured investigations into specific desktop pain points
- **What-If explorations** — Speculative designs and thought experiments
- **Cross-platform comparisons** — How other operating systems handle the same problem
- **Prototype spikes** — Small, focused experiments to test an idea quickly
- **Findings & recommendations** — Actionable output for distros, desktop environments, and upstream projects

## What We Are Not

- A distro or a product company
- An anti-Linux or anti-open-source initiative
- A standards body imposing one "correct" way
- A place for flame wars about DE choices or package formats
